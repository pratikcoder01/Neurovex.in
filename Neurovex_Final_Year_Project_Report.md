## 1. ABSTRACT

Neurovex is an innovative, AI-powered, non-invasive Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) system meticulously designed to assist differently-abled individuals suffering from severe motor impairments. The central purpose of this system is to enable hands-free, autonomous control of wheelchairs and various Internet of Things (IoT) devices by directly bridging the gap between human cognition and electronic actuation. By utilizing electroencephalography (EEG) to capture brainwave patterns, Neurovex eliminates the reliance on traditional physical control mechanisms such as joysticks, mechanical switches, or touch-based interfaces, which often prove unfeasible for patients with advanced paralysis or quadriparesis.

The acquisition of microvolt-level neural activity is achieved through non-invasive scalp electrodes, and the signals are amplified and digitized for real-time analysis. The core of Neurovex integrates dynamic EEG signal processing with Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) to accurately interpret various mental states, most notably focused attention and deep relaxation. Upon successful classification of these cognitive states, the system maps the inferred intent to deterministic hardware commands, enabling localized device control. 

Neurovex ultimately aims to improve accessibility, independence, and overall quality of life by providing an intelligent, safe, and highly accessible assistive solution. The architecture serves as a scalable foundation that extends beyond immediate mobility assistance, charting pathways for widespread integration into modern healthcare, neuro-rehabilitation, academic research, and advanced human-machine interaction ecosystems.

## 2. INTRODUCTION

The emergence of Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) technology marks a paradigm shift in the realm of biomedical engineering and human-computer interaction (HCI). A BCI is a direct communication pathway between an enhanced or wired brain and an external device. Historically, BCI systems were confined strictly to rigorous clinical environments, necessitating the use of cumbersome multi-channel caps, abrasive conductive gels, and massive computational servers to decode neurological telemetry. However, recent advancements in embedded microcontrollers, edge computing, and dry-electrode bio-sensing have drastically democratized the field, transitioning it from theoretical neuroscience to practical assistive technology.

The motivation behind Neurovex is rooted in the critical necessity to provide agency to individuals possessing full cognitive clarity but lacking voluntary muscular control. Current assistive technologies often operate on the assumption of residual mobility. Voice-controlled systems require clear dysarthria-free speech and quiet environments; eye-tracking systems can induce severe ocular fatigue and suffer from the "Midas Touch" problem; and sip-and-puff controls are physically exhausting over continuous usage. 

Neurovex bypasses the physical body entirely. By reading the electrical fluctuations generated by cortical neurons synchronously firing within the brain, the system interprets pure human intent. When a user actively concentrates on a task, the prefrontal cortex exhibits specific high-frequency spectral signatures. Neurovex captures these bio-potentials using a non-invasive EEG headband. The signals are subsequently filtered, amplified, and streamed to an external processing unit where machine learning algorithms classify the user's cognitive state in real-time. This project explores the entire engineering pipeline required to translate these microscopic analog voltages into macroscale mechanical locomotion, explicitly targeting the autonomous navigation of assistive wheelchairs.

## 3. OBJECTIVES OF THE PROJECT

The primary objectives established for the Neurovex project encompass both the fundamental engineering requirements and the broader socio-medical goals:

1. **To design a non-invasive EEG-based Brain-Computer Interface:** Create a user-friendly, wearable sensor framework that can safely and comfortably acquire brainwave data without surgical intervention or the need for clinical skin abrasion.
2. **To enable hands-free control of digital and hardware systems:** Establish a zero-latency, highly deterministic communication pipeline capable of translating neurological states into precise robotic actuation and software navigation.
3. **To analyze EEG signals for focus, relaxation, and fatigue detection:** Implement robust digital signal processing techniques and AI classifiers to filter out electromyographic (muscle) noise and accurately identify Alpha, Beta, and Theta frequency bands associated with specific mental states.
4. **To provide real-time visualization and feedback:** Develop an intuitive, web-based dashboard that grants users and medical supervisors immediate visual telemetry of brain activity, system confidence, and hardware status.
5. **To build a low-cost and scalable assistive solution:** Utilize commercially available, budget-friendly microcontrollers (ESP32) and BioAmp modules to drastically undercut the prohibitive costs of current commercial medical-grade BCI offerings.
6. **To support research, rehabilitation, and accessibility applications:** Design a modular software and hardware architecture that can be easily adapted by hospitals for stroke rehabilitation, or by researchers pushing the boundaries of cognitive science.

## 4. PROBLEM STATEMENT

Globally, millions of individuals endure severe physical and neurological impairments caused by conditions such as Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS), high-level spinal cord injuries, severe cerebral palsy, and locked-in syndrome. These demographics face monumental challenges in operating standard motorized wheelchairs, fundamentally stripping them of their autonomy. 

Traditional wheelchair control mechanisms overwhelmingly rely on joysticks or physical switches. For an individual lacking the motor coordination or muscle strength to grasp a joystick, these chairs become useless. Alternative solutions exist but carry significant drawbacks. Voice-activated systems suffer severely in ambiently noisy environments and are entirely ineffective for non-verbal users or those with speech impairments. Sip-and-puff mechanisms, while popular, are unhygienic, socially stigmatizing, and require immense respiratory effort over prolonged durations.

Furthermore, while commercial Brain-Computer Interface wheelchairs have been prototyped in research labs, they remain astronomically expensive, highly invasive, and often require a team of engineers to calibrate and operate perfectly in real-world environments. 

Therefore, there is an urgent and critical need for the development of a low-cost, non-invasive, intelligent, and reliable EEG-based assistive system. This system must enable completely hands-free wheelchair mobility while ensuring stringent safety protocols, maintaining high classification efficiency, and offering real-time cognitive state monitoring to genuinely enhance user independence and day-to-day mobility without imposing a heavy financial or operational burden.

## 5. PROPOSED SYSTEM / METHODOLOGY

The Neurovex methodology represents a complete horizontal integration of biosignal acquisition, network transmission, cloud integration, and algorithmic processing. 

**Working Pipeline Step-By-Step:**

1. **EEG Signal Capture:** The process begins securely at the user level. Using a non-invasive EEG band equipped with Penta Gel Electrodes, the system makes contact with the user's scalp (specifically targeting the prefrontal cortex via the Fp1 position). As the user engages in varying cognitive tasks, microscopic analog voltage changes are detected.
2. **Signal Amplification (BioAmp EXG Pill):** Raw brainwaves are measured in microvolts (µV) and are easily corrupted by electromagnetic interference (like 50Hz/60Hz AC noise from wall outlets). The BioAmp EXG Pill acts as the critical analog front-end, applying differential amplification and bandpass filtering to enlarge the target frequencies while drastically suppressing common-mode noise.
3. **Data Acquisition using ESP32:** The amplified, clean analog signal is fed into the high-resolution Analog-to-Digital Converter (ADC) of the ESP32 microcontroller. The ESP32 digitizes the electrical waveform at a rapid 250Hz sampling rate, ensuring compliance with the Nyquist theorem for capturing high-frequency Gamma and Beta waves.
4. **Data Transmission:** The ESP32 packages the digitized integer payloads and streams them in real-time to a local processing unit (a laptop or onboard computer) over a rapid Web Serial interface or WebSocket connection.
5. **Signal Processing:** The backend processing layer applies a Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) and temporal variance analysis to break the complex raw wave down into its constituent frequency bands (Delta, Theta, Alpha, Beta, Gamma). 
6. **AI Pattern Detection:** A machine learning model analyzes the spectral density and band power of the signal. If the power ratio leans heavily toward the Beta band combined with sustained amplitude, the AI classifies the user's state as "Focused." If it shifts toward the Alpha band, it is registered as "Relaxed." 
7. **Dashboard Visualization:** The processed states, along with a live rendering of the raw waveform and signal quality confidence metrics, are pushed to the frontend HTML/JS dashboard for supervision and neuro-feedback gamification.
8. **Hardware Control Commands:** The interpreted cognitive states are routed to the central hardware decision system. If the "Focus" state surpasses a strict numerical threshold for a sustained validation period, the system issues a definitive actuation command (e.g., `"FORWARD"`). This command acts as a relay, bridging the software realm to the L298N motor driver, completing the cycle by spinning the wheels of the assistive wheelchair.

## 6. SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

The Neurovex system architecture is designed around a decoupled, modular framework to maximize fault tolerance and minimize end-to-end latency.

### **System Block Diagram**

```text
[ Physical Brain ] 
       │
       ▼ (Analog Microvolts)
[ Penta Gel Electrodes ] ───> [ BioAmp EXG Pill ] (Amplification & Filtering)
                                     │
                                     ▼ (0-3.3V Analog)
                              [ ESP32 Microcontroller ] (ADC & Serialization)
                                     │
                                     ▼ (Digital Data Stream - USB/WiFi)
[ LOCAL PROCESSING NODE (Backend) ]
       │
       ├──> [ Signal Processing Module ] (FFT & Filtering)
       │           │
       │           ▼
       ├──> [ AI Pattern Detection ] (ML Classifier)
       │           │
       │           ├──> [ User Interface / Web Dashboard ] (Data Visualization)
       │           │
       │           └──> [ Supabase Cloud ] (Data Storage & Auth)
       │
       ▼ (Control Logic)
[ Safety & Hardware Control Module ]
       │
       ▼ (PWM / Digital Signals)
[ Motor Driver Circuit ]
       │
       ▼
[ Assistive Device / Wheelchair Chassis ]
```

**Architecture Explanation:**
The architecture operates linearly from biologically generated analog data down to mechanical kinetic data. The biological sensors interface locally via the BioAmp Pill to the node (ESP32). The ESP32 guarantees high-fidelity, uninterrupted streaming to prevent data bottlenecks. The local processing node offloads the mathematically expensive tasks (like FFT execution) away from the microcontroller to maintain high-speed sampling. Once the ML models predict the user intent, the control loop splits: visualization data telemetry goes up to the Cloud and UI, while mission-critical mechanical actuation commands bypass the cloud and go straight back out to the real-time hardware control modules.

## 7. HARDWARE DESCRIPTION

The Neurovex physical framework consists of carefully selected, synergistic electronic components.

| Component | Purpose / Working Principle | Justification for Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **EEG Band** | A structurally flexible band that holds the electrodes flush against the scalp at targeted 10-20 international system locations. | Ensures continuous physical contact required to log sub-dermal voltage fluctuations without movement artifact interference. |
| **Penta Gel Electrodes** | Wet/Gel-based medical adhesive electrodes that lower skin impedance. | Essential for penetrating the dead-skin layer barrier to acquire a low-noise, clinical-grade brainwave signal. |
| **BioAmp EXG Pill** | A miniature, high-performance biopotential analog front-end. It features an instrumentation amplifier with a massive Common Mode Rejection Ratio (CMRR). | Brainwaves are minuscule compared to muscle electricity and ambient EMF. The BioAmp pill isolates the pure brainwave and scales the voltage linearly to be readable by basic microcontrollers. |
| **BioAmp Cable v3** | Shielded interconnect cables ensuring signal integrity between the electrodes and the BioAmp Pill. | Unshielded cables act as antennas for rogue electromagnetic noise. The v3 cable protects the microvolt analog signals during transit. |
| **ESP32** | A powerful 32-bit dual-core microcontroller equipped with a 12-bit Analog-to-Digital Converter (ADC). It reads the BioAmp output 250 times a second and serializes it. | Chosen for its superior clock speed (240MHz) and high ADC resolution over older microcontrollers (like Arduino Uno), plus embedded BLE/WiFi capabilities for future wireless implementations. |

## 8. SOFTWARE DESCRIPTION / TECHNOLOGY STACK

Neurovex adopts a modern, decoupled web-stack approach, fusing rapid web telemetry with robust backend machine-learning analytics.

**Frontend Layer (HTML, CSS, JavaScript):**
The client-facing dashboard is constructed using vanilla HTML/JS paired with modern CSS frameworks (Tailwind) to ensure a lightweight footprint. It utilizes the HTML5 Canvas API to render real-time, 60 FPS flowing electroencephalogram waveforms. The UI operates directly within a browser, completely platform-agnostic, and avoids the overhead of thick desktop applications.

**Backend Layer (Python, FastAPI):**
FastAPI operates as the asynchronous backend server. Due to its foundation on Starlette and Pydantic, it handles thousands of incoming EEG data points gracefully without blocking threads. Python is selected as the backend language specifically because of its unrivaled ecosystem for data science and array manipulation (NumPy, SciPy).

**Signal Processing Layer (FFT & Band Power):**
Residing predominantly on the backend, this layer takes the raw integer arrays and subjects them to Fast Fourier Transforms. This converts the signal from the Time Domain into the Frequency Domain, allowing the system to isolate and calculate the absolute "Band Power" of specific ranges (e.g., how much total energy is strictly between 13Hz and 30Hz).

**AI System Layer (ML-Based EEG Classification):**
Classical ML algorithms (such as Support Vector Machines or Random Forests) are continuously fed the extracted Band Power features generated by the signal processing layer. The models are pre-trained on standardized empirical datasets linking specific spectral topographies to states of high-focus versus relaxed resting-state networks.

**Database Layer (Supabase):**
Supabase, an open-source Firebase alternative powered by PostgreSQL, provides the backbone for structured data persistence. It facilitates JWT-based Row-Level-Security (RLS) authentication, ensuring that patient telemetry is securely sandboxed. Deep history logs, processed session analytics, and user metadata are continuously pushed via REST APIs to the cloud.

**Communication Layer (HTTP & WebSockets):**
For static data (login, fetching historical sessions), standard HTTP REST protocols are executed. However, for live hardware manipulation and real-time graph rendering, the architecture utilizes bidirectional WebSockets and the Web Serial API for instantaneous, sub-10ms data transmission between the hardware, backend server, and browser UI.

## 9. MODULES DESCRIPTION

Neurovex is segmented into six distinct responsibilities, operating cohesively:

1. **EEG Acquisition Module:** Functions purely to acquire data. It encapsulates the electrodes, BioAmp EXG Pill, and ESP32. It cares nothing for logic or visualization; its sole duty is keeping the precise 250Hz timing cadence and producing accurate raw analog arrays.
2. **Signal Processing Module:** The mathematics engine. It receives noisy integer buffers and passes them through software-based bandpass filters (e.g., 1Hz to 50Hz) and Notch filters (to eliminate 50Hz mains power hum). It outputs clean, distinct frequency bins.
3. **AI Pattern Detection Module:** The cognitive discriminator. It consumes the clean frequency bins. Utilizing its trained weights, it produces a probabilistic array determining the exact mental state (Focus: 85%, Relax: 15%) at any given continuous second.
4. **Safety & Hardware Control Module:** The physical executor and watchdog. It takes the output of the AI module and applies threshold logic. If Focus > Threshold, it commands the motor driver. Crucially, it monitors signal variance; if the electrodes disconnect (creating a massive impedance spike), the safety logic instantly interlocks the motors, triggering an emergency stop.
5. **Data Storage Module:** The archivist. It groups the continuous 250Hz data streams into "Sessions" and commits them to the PostgreSQL database in Supabase, along with average session metrics for historical tracking.
6. **User Interface Module:** The bridge for human interaction. It surfaces the complex ML data into digestible visualizations: radial progress bars for fatigue, live charts for raw waves, and administrative panels for configuring safety thresholds.

## 10. SIGNAL PROCESSING

Understanding raw EEG signals requires breaking them down into biologically established rhythm bands.

### **EEG Frequency Bands**
- **Delta (1 - 4 Hz):** The slowest, highest amplitude brainwaves. Predominantly generated during deep, dreamless, non-REM sleep. In wakeful adults, high delta often indicates brain damage or severe cognitive fatigue.
- **Theta (4 - 8 Hz):** Associated with light sleep, deep meditative states, and internal subconscious focus.
- **Alpha (8 - 13 Hz):** The bridge between conscious thought and the subconscious. Alpha waves spike dramatically when an individual sits quietly with their eyes closed, resting in a relaxed but awake state.
- **Beta (13 - 30 Hz):** The primary frequency of waking consciousness. Beta waves dominate when an individual is highly alert, engaged in active problem solving, physical movement planning, or intense focused concentration.
- **Gamma (30 - 100 Hz):** The fastest waves, linking simultaneous processing of information from different brain areas, representing high-level cognitive functioning, perception, and consciousness.

### **Analytical Mechanics**
**FFT Analysis:** Fast Fourier Transform is applied mathematically to a rolling window (e.g., 1 second of data, or 250 data points). The output is a spectrogram showing the amplitude of all frequencies concurrently existing within that second.
**Band Power Calculation:** The integral of the power spectral density curve within specific frequency cutoff limits (e.g., 13Hz to 30Hz) determines the "Beta Band Power." 
**Focus Detection:** Focus is inferred by calculating the ratio between high-frequency Beta band power and low-frequency Alpha/Theta band power. An elevated Beta/Alpha ratio strongly indicates an actively engaging prefrontal cortex.
**Relaxation Detection:** Conversely, if the system detects a suppression of Beta waves accompanied by a towering amplitude spike in the 10Hz Alpha range, the user is classified as deeply relaxed.

## 11. DASHBOARD SYSTEM

The web-based visualization layer provides a critical feedback loop for both the user and researchers. Operating over a local or cloud-hosted web server, the dashboard provides a multitude of components:

- **Real-Time EEG Waveform:** Utilizing the Canvas API, the dashboard renders the raw analog voltage fluctuation as a continuously scrolling oscilloscope trace. The system applies auto-centering algorithms to prevent the wave from drifting off-screen due to galvanic skin response fluctuations.
- **Focus Meter:** A real-time, circular progress gauge operating from 0 to 100%. As the backend AI detects higher Beta ratios, the gauge fills smoothly using Exponential Moving Average (EMA) mathematical smoothing to prevent visually jarring jumps.
- **Signal Quality Monitoring (Confidence):** A proprietary metric that detects Electromyography (EMG) noise (like jaw clenching or blinking) or extreme impedance shifts. If raw variance exceeds biological limits, Confidence drops to 0%, warning the user that telemetry is compromised.
- **Session Recording & Visualization:** Users can initialize timestamped recording blocks. The dashboard captures all telemetry for the duration and, upon completion, generates local charts and synchronized Supabase data logs analyzing the user’s cognitive stamina over time.

## 12. HARDWARE CONTROL SYSTEM

Connecting the cognitive state to mechanical reality is the foundation of Neurovex’s assistive capability. 

### **The Decision Chain**
1. **Focus Detection:** The AI module outputs an ongoing variable `FocusScore` (e.g., 75%).
2. **Decision System:** Setting a static threshold is not viable due to human neuroplasticity. Therefore, users set a personalized "Trigger Threshold" (e.g., 60%) inside the dashboard.
3. **Movement Commands:** When `FocusScore >= Trigger Threshold` for a sustained period (e.g., 1000 consecutive milliseconds to prevent accidental misfires), the system generates a definitive string command, such as `"FORWARD"`. This is relayed down via the Web Serial API to the ESP32, which drives the L298N module Pins HIGH/LOW, commanding the motors to advance the wheelchair.
4. **Safety Stop Mechanism:** A "dead-man's switch" ideology is baked into the architecture. The hardware strictly defaults to an idle, stopped state. If the `FocusScore` dips back below the threshold, or if the user generates a sudden Alpha spike (deliberate relaxation), or if the Signal Confidence drops due to a slipped electrode, the system instantly overwrites the command buffer with `"STOP"`, engaging motor braking protocols.

## 15. LIMITATIONS

- **Signal Noise:** The human skull is an excellent insulator. Filtering out 50Hz mains power, muscle clenching (EMG), and eye-blinking (EOG) to view pure microvolt brainwaves requires immense algorithmic tuning.
- **AI Model Accuracy:** The current ML classifiers generalize intent. "Focusing on a math problem" and "Focusing on driving" both simply output high Beta waves. Nuanced directional intent ("Think Left" vs "Think Right") requires vastly more advanced deep-learning convolutional networks and multi-channel hardware arrays.
- **Limited Testing Environments:** Current deployment strategies have relied on controlled, relatively quiet environments. Busy, extremely electrically noisy industrial environments may introduce unpredictable electromagnetic interference resulting in dropped signal confidence.
- **Hardware Constraints:** Relying on single-channel (Fp1) mapping inherently limits spatial resolution. We observe global prefrontal activity, but we cannot triangulate specific sub-cortical origins without expanding to a 16 or 32-channel clinical headset.
